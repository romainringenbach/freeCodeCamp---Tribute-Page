Ryoji Ikeda

Career

Born in 1996, Ryoji Ikeda's career begin with audiovisual production in Japan. In the early 2000' he joined the artist collective Dumb Type and won the Golden Nica Award. Since his beginning, he released multiple recordings, ep and albums. He performed on stage numerous times and created audio-visual expositions all around the world. He also resided to the CERN after winning the Prix Arts Electronica in 2014.

Description of its art

Tacking multiple mediums, from recordings to audio-visual installations in the world, the art of Ryoji Ikeda relies on beat patterns, maths, and data. He creates minimal music from raw electronic sounds, giving a sense of slowly evolving soundscapes. Made from data visualization and mathematical functions, his visual work immerses his audience inside his installation and plays with lights.
